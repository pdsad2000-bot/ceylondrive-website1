Frequently Asked Questions

Clear, honest answers to the questions Sri Lankan drivers ask most.

General Questions

Do I legally need motor insurance in Sri Lanka? ❓
Yes. Under the Motor Traffic Act (No. 14 of 1951) and its amendments, every motor vehicle on a public road in Sri Lanka must have at least a valid Third-Party Liability insurance policy. Driving without insurance can result in a fine, vehicle impoundment, and even licence suspension. Our Basic Plan satisfies this legal requirement at the lowest possible annual cost.
What is the difference between Third-Party and Comprehensive insurance? ❓
Third-Party insurance only covers damage or injury you cause to other people and their property. It does not pay for damage to your own vehicle.

Comprehensive insurance covers everything in Third-Party, plus damage to your own vehicle, theft, fire, and personal accident. If your car is involved in an accident — even if you are at fault — comprehensive insurance covers your repair costs too.
How quickly can I get my insurance certificate after payment? ❓
Once your payment is confirmed, your Certificate of Insurance (Cover Note) is issued within 24 hours on working days. We send it to your email and WhatsApp. A printed copy can be collected from our Colombo office. The cover note is the legal document you must carry in your vehicle.

Claims Questions

What should I do immediately after a road accident? ❓
Follow these steps in order:

1. Ensure safety first — move to a safe location if possible and check for injuries.
2. Call 119 (Police Emergency) to report the accident.
3. Call CeylonDrive on 011-123-4567 immediately — our 24/7 team will guide you.
4. Take photos of vehicle damage, the scene, and the other party's licence plate.
5. Do not admit fault at the scene — let our claims team handle liability.
6. Visit the nearest police station to obtain the Motor Traffic Accident Report.
How long does it take to settle a claim? ❓
For straightforward claims with complete documentation, we aim to settle within 7 working days. Complex or disputed claims may take up to 21 working days depending on the police report, surveyor's assessment, and third-party involvement. We will keep you informed at every step via phone or WhatsApp.
What documents do I need to submit for a claim? ❓
You will need to submit the following documents:

• Completed Claim Form (available from our office or by WhatsApp)
• Motor Traffic Accident Report (from police station)
• Valid Driving Licence (photocopy)
• Vehicle Registration Document (Revenue Licence / CR Book)
• Insurance Certificate (Cover Note or Policy Document)
• Photographs of the vehicle damage and accident scene
• Repair Estimate from a recognised garage (for own-damage claims)

Premiums & Renewals

What is a No-Claim Bonus and how much can I save? ❓
A No-Claim Bonus (NCB) is a discount you earn on your renewal premium for every year you do not make a claim. Here is how it works at CeylonDrive:

• After 1 claim-free year: 10% discount
• After 2 claim-free years: 20% discount
• After 3 claim-free years: 30% discount (Standard) / 40% (Premium)
• After 4+ claim-free years: up to 50% discount (Premium plan)

The NCB is transferable if you switch to a new vehicle, provided you show proof from your previous insurer.
